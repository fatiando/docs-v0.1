Processing and transformations using the FFT (fatiando.gravmag.fourier)¶

Potential field processing using the Fast Fourier Transform

Note

Requires gridded data to work!

Derivatives

  • derivx: Calculate the n-th order derivative of a potential field in the x-direction
  • derivy: Calculate the n-th order derivative of a potential field in the y-direction
  • derivz: Calculate the n-th order derivative of a potential field in the z-direction
fatiando.gravmag.fourier.derivx(x, y, data, shape, order=1)[source]¶

Calculate the derivative of a potential field in the x direction.

Warning

If the data is not in SI units, the derivative will be in strange units! I strongly recommend converting the data to SI before calculating the derivative (use one of the unit conversion functions of fatiando.utils). This way the derivative will be in SI units and can be easily converted to what unit you want.

Parameters:

  • x, y : 1D-arrays

    The x and y coordinates of the grid points

  • data : 1D-array

    The potential field at the grid points

  • shape : tuple = (ny, nx)

    The shape of the grid

  • order : int

    The order of the derivative

Returns:

  • deriv : 1D-array

    The derivative
